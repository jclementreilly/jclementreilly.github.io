Remembrance Scholarship

One of the greatest honors I have ever been a part of is the Remembrance Program during my undergrad at Syracuse University. In 1988, thirty-five Syracuse students out of 270 were killed in the bombing of Pan Am Flight 103. Each year, 35 Remembrance Scholarships are awarded to senior undergraduate students on the basis of distinguished academic achievement, citizenship, and service to community. Scholars organize events for Remembrance Week in October and carry on the legacy of those who lost their lives. I look back and act forward in memory of Julianne F. Kelly, a woman whom I hope to one day name my daughter after.
