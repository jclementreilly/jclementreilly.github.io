About Me

Design is the practice of crafting the world around you. Above all else, I do this.

My name is Jacqueline, but my friends call me Jackie. You can too.

Much to my mother's surprise, my childhood mascara paintings on her bathroom sink and the green crayon designs on the wall were a sign of great things to come — I am a natural-born creative. After receiving my Bachelor of Science from the Newhouse School of Communications at Syracuse University, I began my career with IBM Design. There I learned that everything we create is a prototype, including ourselves. I then served as a design strategist at Pegasystems, where I helped scale the practice of design. I am in a new phase now: some call it unemployment; I call it unlimited, unpaid vacation time. I am spending it by iterating on my next design challenge.

As I consistently iterate myself and my career, I seek to have an impact on the way human beings interact with their own health and wellness. If I sound like someone you'd like to grab a matcha, green juice, or a good craft brew with, connect with me.
